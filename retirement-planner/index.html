    This run vs previous re-runs: → small variation between runs is the honest randomness; the answer stays close to the true probability.
    Why does the % barely change when I re-run?

    Because that's your plan's true success rate, computed honestly. Each individual simulated future is random — the 5 samples above re-shuffle on every run, you can verify by clicking Re-run with different luck. But their average, across 10,000 futures, converges. With only 100 sims the number would jump from 40% to 60% between runs; with 10,000 it settles within ±1% of the truth. That stability isn't proof it's fake — it's proof it's working.

    Think of it like flipping a fair coin 10,000 times: you'll always get close to 50% heads, never exactly. We're flipping 10,000 "market futures" against your plan; the % you see is your plan's honest survival rate.
